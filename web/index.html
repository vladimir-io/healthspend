The Mission

For decades, healthcare pricing operated in a black box. When transparency first became law, institutions responded with unreadable datasets. The data was deliberately obfuscated.

A 2026 report by the Purchaser Business Group on Health isolated the incentive. Insurance negotiated rates are frequently higher than what a patient would pay in cash. Opacity protects the margin.

On April 1, 2026, the regulatory landscape shifted. The CY 2026 CMS Final Rule mandates that every hospital publish actual dollar amounts in standardized, machine readable templates.

Healthspend was engineered to enforce this mandate.

The terminal continuously audits over 7,400 US hospital nodes against the federal schema. It extracts exact cash rates, logs regulatory noncompliance, and provides the programmatic leverage necessary for patients to instantly route federal complaints.

Healthspend serves as the deterministic data layer required to force a transparent healthcare market.
